Book-Based ECG Scenario Atlas

12-Lead Placement and Wall Map

Limb Leads

I: LA+ / RA−. II: LL+ / RA−. III: LL+ / LA−. Inferior wall is II, III, aVF. Lateral wall is I, aVL.

Augmented Leads

aVR usually negative. aVL left high lateral. aVF inferior. Use the hexaxial system to judge axis.

Precordial Leads

V1 4th ICS right sternal border. V2 4th ICS left sternal. V4 5th ICS midclavicular. V5-V6 lateral.

V1–V3

Right ventricle and septum. Useful for RBBB, anterior ischemia, Brugada-like patterns, and posterior reciprocal clues.

V4–V6

V4 apex; V5–V6 lateral wall. Watch R-wave progression and transition zone around V3–V4.

Extra Leads

V7–V9 posterior wall. V3R–V4R right ventricle. Useful when inferior STEMI or posterior MI is suspected.

Expert Interpretation Pathway

1
Quality

Name, calibration 10 mm/mV, speed 25 mm/s, artifact, lead placement.

2
Rhythm

Sinus? P before every QRS? Regular? Escape or ectopic?

3
Intervals

PR 120–200 ms, QRS normally 60–100 ms, QTc usually <440 ms adult.

4
Morphology

Axis, hypertrophy, R progression, bundle/fascicular blocks.

5
Ischemia

J point, ST segment, T waves, reciprocal changes, clinical symptoms.

Memory rule: “Regular P, normal PR, narrow QRS” = think sinus. “No P + irregularly irregular” = think AF. “Wide and fast” = VT until proven otherwise. “ST elevation + symptoms” = emergency escalation.

NHA EKG Exam Tips

Must-memorize values

Paper speed 25 mm/s. Small box = 0.04 s. Large box = 0.20 s. PR = 0.12–0.20 s. QRS usually <0.12 s. Sinus = 60–100 bpm.

Danger rhythms

V-Fib, pulseless V-Tach, asystole, complete heart block with instability, STEMI pattern with symptoms, severe hyperkalemia.

Similar rhythm traps

AF is irregularly irregular. Flutter has sawtooth F waves. SVT is usually very regular and narrow. VT is usually wide and dangerous.

AV block ladder

1st degree: long PR. Wenckebach: longer, longer, drop. Mobitz II: fixed PR then drop. 3rd degree: P and QRS unrelated.

Lead placement trap

Wrong V1/V2 placement can mimic infarct or bundle changes. Always check lead placement before overcalling pathology.

Clinical safety

In exams and real care, unstable patient signs matter: chest pain, syncope, hypotension, altered mental status, shock, poor perfusion.
